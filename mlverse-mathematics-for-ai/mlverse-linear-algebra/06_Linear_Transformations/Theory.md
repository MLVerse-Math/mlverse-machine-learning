# Theory of Linear Transformations

## Definition

A linear transformation is a function:

\[
T:V\rightarrow W
\]

that satisfies:

---

## Additivity

\[
T(u+v)=T(u)+T(v)
\]

---

## Homogeneity

\[
T(cu)=cT(u)
\]

where:

- u,v are vectors
- c is a scalar

---

## Matrix Representation

Every linear transformation can be represented as:

\[
T(x)=Ax
\]

where:

- A is a matrix
- x is a vector

---

## Examples

### Scaling

\[
A=
\begin{bmatrix}
2&0\\
0&2
\end{bmatrix}
\]

### Rotation

\[
A=
\begin{bmatrix}
\cos\theta&-\sin\theta\\
\sin\theta&\cos\theta
\end{bmatrix}
\]

### Reflection

\[
A=
\begin{bmatrix}
1&0\\
0&-1
\end{bmatrix}
\]

### Shear

\[
A=
\begin{bmatrix}
1&k\\
0&1
\end{bmatrix}
\]
