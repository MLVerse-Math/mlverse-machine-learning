# Theory of Vector Spaces

## What is a Vector Space?

A vector space is a collection of vectors that satisfies specific mathematical rules under:

1. Vector Addition
2. Scalar Multiplication

---

## Example

The set:

\[
\mathbb{R}^2
\]

contains all vectors:

\[
(x,y)
\]

and forms a vector space.

---

## Properties of Vector Spaces

A vector space must satisfy:

- Closure under Addition
- Closure under Scalar Multiplication
- Associativity
- Commutativity
- Additive Identity
- Additive Inverse

---
