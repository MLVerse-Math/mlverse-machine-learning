# Mathematics of Vector Spaces

## Linear Combination

Given vectors:

\[
v_1,v_2,\ldots,v_n
\]

A linear combination is:

\[
a_1v_1+a_2v_2+\cdots+a_nv_n
\]

---

## Span

The span of vectors is the set of all possible linear combinations.

\[
Span(v_1,v_2)
\]

---

## Subspace

A subset of a vector space that is itself a vector space.

Conditions:

- Contains Zero Vector
- Closed under Addition
- Closed under Scalar Multiplication

---

## Basis

A basis is a set of vectors that:

- Are Linearly Independent
- Span the Entire Space

Example:

\[
\left\{
\begin{bmatrix}
1\\
0
\end{bmatrix},
\begin{bmatrix}
0\\
1
\end{bmatrix}
\right\}
\]

---

## Dimension

Dimension is the number of vectors in a basis.

Examples:

\[
dim(\mathbb{R}^2)=2
\]

\[
dim(\mathbb{R}^3)=3
\]

---

## Coordinate Representation

Any vector can be represented as a combination of basis vectors.

Example:

\[
\begin{bmatrix}
3\\
5
\end{bmatrix}
=
3
\begin{bmatrix}
1\\
0
\end{bmatrix}

- 5
  \begin{bmatrix}
  0\\
  1
  \end{bmatrix}
  \]
