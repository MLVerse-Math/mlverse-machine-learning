# Mathematics of Matrices

# Matrix Addition

For matrices:

\[
A=
\begin{bmatrix}
1&2\\
3&4
\end{bmatrix}
\]

\[
B=
\begin{bmatrix}
5&6\\
7&8
\end{bmatrix}
\]

Then:

\[
A+B=
\begin{bmatrix}
6&8\\
10&12
\end{bmatrix}
\]

---

# Matrix Subtraction

\[
A-B
\]

performed element-wise.

---

# Scalar Multiplication

\[
kA
\]

Example:

\[
3
\begin{bmatrix}
1&2\\
3&4
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
3&6\\
9&12
\end{bmatrix}
\]

---

# Matrix Multiplication

If:

\[
A\_{m\times n}
\]

and

\[
B\_{n\times p}
\]

Then:

\[
C=A\times B
\]

Dimension:

\[
m\times p
\]

---

Example

\[
A=
\begin{bmatrix}
1&2\\
3&4
\end{bmatrix}
\]

\[
B=
\begin{bmatrix}
5&6\\
7&8
\end{bmatrix}
\]

Result:

\[
AB=
\begin{bmatrix}
19&22\\
43&50
\end{bmatrix}
\]

---

# Matrix Transpose

\[
A^T
\]

Rows become columns.

---

Example

\[
\begin{bmatrix}
1&2\\
3&4
\end{bmatrix}
\]

becomes

\[
\begin{bmatrix}
1&3\\
2&4
\end{bmatrix}
\]

---

# Determinant

For:

\[
A=
\begin{bmatrix}
a&b\\
c&d
\end{bmatrix}
\]

Determinant:

\[
det(A)=ad-bc
\]

---

# Inverse Matrix

\[
A^{-1}
\]

exists if:

\[
det(A)\neq0
\]

---

# Rank

Rank represents:

The number of linearly independent rows or columns.

---

# Key Takeaways

- Matrices store data.
- Matrix multiplication powers AI.
- Determinants determine invertibility.
- Transpose changes orientation.
