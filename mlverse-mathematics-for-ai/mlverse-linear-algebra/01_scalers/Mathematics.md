# 🔢 Mathematics of Scalars

# Definition

A scalar is a quantity represented by a single numerical value.

Mathematically:

\[
a \in \mathbb{R}
\]

where:

- \(a\) is a scalar
- \(\mathbb{R}\) is the set of real numbers

Examples:

\[
5,\quad -3,\quad 0,\quad 2.7,\quad \pi
\]

---

# Scalar Arithmetic

## Addition

Given two scalars:

\[
a,b \in \mathbb{R}
\]

Addition is defined as:

\[
a+b
\]

Example:

\[
5+3=8
\]

---

## Subtraction

\[
a-b
\]

Example:

\[
8-2=6
\]

---

## Multiplication

\[
ab
\]

Example:

\[
4\times3=12
\]

---

## Division

\[
\frac{a}{b}
\]

where

\[
b \neq 0
\]

Example:

\[
\frac{10}{2}=5
\]

---

# Properties of Scalars

## Closure Property

For any

\[
a,b \in \mathbb{R}
\]

then

\[
a+b \in \mathbb{R}
\]

and

\[
ab \in \mathbb{R}
\]

---

## Commutative Property

Addition:

\[
a+b=b+a
\]

Multiplication:

\[
ab=ba
\]

Example:

\[
2+5=5+2
\]

\[
2\times5=5\times2
\]

---

## Associative Property

Addition:

\[
(a+b)+c=a+(b+c)
\]

Multiplication:

\[
(ab)c=a(bc)
\]

---

## Distributive Property

\[
a(b+c)=ab+ac
\]

Example:

\[
2(3+4)=6+8
\]

---

# Scalar Multiplication

Scalars can scale vectors.

Given

\[
k=3
\]

and vector

\[
v=
\begin{bmatrix}
1\\
2\\
3
\end{bmatrix}
\]

Then

\[
kv=
3
\begin{bmatrix}
1\\
2\\
3
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
3\\
6\\
9
\end{bmatrix}
\]

---

# Norm of a Scalar

For a scalar

\[
a
\]

the magnitude is:

\[
|a|
\]

Example:

\[
|-5|=5
\]

---

# Scalar in Coordinate Geometry

A scalar can represent:

- Distance
- Time
- Temperature
- Mass
- Energy

Example:

Distance:

\[
d=50m
\]

Temperature:

\[
T=37^\circ C
\]
